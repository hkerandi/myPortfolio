Carceral Algorithms Research

Data storytelling & ethical analysis

This project examined discrepancies in parole recommendation algorithms using anonymized open datasets. I investigated how race correlated with risk scores, release decisions, and error rates.

Methods

  • Python (Pandas, NumPy), data cleaning & pipeline design
  • Distribution analysis & pairwise correlations
  • Model fairness metrics (FPR, FNR, precision)
  • Ethical interpretability using contextual narrative framing

Key Findings

Clear disparities existed between demographic groups, with inflated risk predictions in specific populations.

Spotify Hot 100: Emotional Trends

Cultural analytics • Sentiment modeling • Behavioral insight

This project explores emotional and lyrical trends in North American Hot 100 chart-toppers (2010–2024). Using sentiment analysis, theme clustering, and frequency modeling, I examined how mainstream music reflects shifting cultural moods.

Key Findings

  • Self-love is the fastest growing theme — empowerment and autonomy increasingly dominate the charts.
  • Serenades & heartache remain the most stable emotional categories across all years.
  • Emotional narratives are more complex — songs now blend grief, empowerment, nostalgia, and confidence.
  • Cultural shift observed: from dependency → autonomy, and from heartbreak → personal healing.

Technical Approach

  • Python (Pandas, NLTK/TextBlob) for sentiment + theme classification
  • Cleaning + structuring multi-year datasets
  • Trend modeling across emotional categories
  • Visualization of long-term shifts in narrative tone

Why It Matters

Music mirrors cultural psychology. The rise of self-love narratives reflects broader social shifts around identity, autonomy, mental health, and gender empowerment. This analysis demonstrates my ability to translate behavioral data into meaningful cultural stories — essential for roles in music tech, personalization, and content intelligence.

Scotland Slavery Legacies

Data storytelling, ethical analysis, qualitative data & research

Why It Matters

Many people move through places without truly knowing their historical significance. Others pass through the same spaces and feel something: an unspoken sense that something happened there, whether the experience was good or bad. Scotland has chosen to confront the skeletons in her closet by educating her population about the role she played in slavery and colonialism. This project marked my introduction to storytelling from a data-driven perspective. This is sensitive information, particularly because there are still living populations affected by the legacy of slavery and colonialism. For that reason, I worked alongside a team to present the data in a way that avoids retraumatization or placing blame on the descendants of either party. Instead, the goal was to provide historical context—what happened at the time, and how those events continue to shape society today. As my professor often says, “History is now.”
